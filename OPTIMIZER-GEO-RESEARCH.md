## Core Concept: GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

**Definition:** Getting your business cited in AI search answers (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) rather than just ranking in traditional Google search.

**Goal:** Be part of the answer itself, not just on page 1 of Google.

## What Actually Impacts AI Visibility: Top 3 Factors

### 1. Brand Mention Authority (3x more important than backlinks)
- How often a business is discussed across the web
- Directory listings, business awards, vendor mentions
- Trade publication mentions
- Professional association listings
- Customer reviews and citations

**Action:** This is what Optimizer targets directly.

### 2. Content Freshness (Critical)
- 50% of citations are <13 weeks old
- AI actively surfaces new/updated content
- Stale content gets deprioritized
- Recommend clients update website content every 3 months minimum

**Action:** Include content update strategy in Optimizer offering.

### 3. Content Structure (For AI Extraction)
- Clear heading hierarchy (H2s, H3s)
- Bullet points and short paragraphs
- Direct, concise answers
- Scannable formatting

**Connection to Answer Capsules:** Answer capsules are perfect for this — they're inherently structured for AI extraction.

## Directory Strategy: Quality Over Quantity

### The Math
- **30 high-authority directories** = better than 300 average
- Cost: 30 directories × $2 = $60/month + base fee ($39-79) = $100-120/month
- Cost: 300 directories = $600/month + base fee = $650/month

### Prioritization Strategy
1. **Industry-specific directories** (highest impact)
   - Example: For plumber → local plumbing associations, Angi, HomeAdvisor
   - These attract both customers AND AI model crawlers

2. **High-authority generics** (strong authority signal)
   - Google Business Profile (tied to Gemini)
   - Yelp (trusted by all AI models)
   - BBB (authority signal)

3. **Avoid generic listings** (low impact)
   - Low-authority business directories
   - Directories with no customer traffic

### Why This Matters
- Being in 1 trade publication + 3 industry directories > 10 random directories
- AI prioritizes "conversational authority" — sources that are trusted and frequently cited
- Benford's Law: Top sources get cited at rates that dwarf everything below

## Platform Comparison: BrightLocal vs Synup

### BrightLocal
- **Pricing:** $39-79/mo base + $2/citation
- **Integrations:** 100+ directories
- **Approach:** Self-service platform, bulk submission
- **Best for:** SMBs wanting to manage themselves

### Synup
- **Pricing:** Custom (enterprise)
- **Integrations:** 100+ directories
- **Approach:** Fully white-label, partner model
- **Best for:** Agencies doing this at scale

### N8N Alternative
- **Approach:** Build custom workflow using directory APIs directly
- **Cost:** Lower per-citation but requires development
- **Risk:** Maintaining integrations as directory APIs change
- **Timeline:** 2-3x longer to build vs using existing platform

**Decision point:** Can't choose until hypothesis is validated with test client.
