# Surven — Rebranding Strategy

*Created: 2026-04-26*

---

## The Problem With the Old Direction

Most AI companies look like AI companies — dark gradients, neon accents, abstract generative visuals, buzzword-heavy copy. It signals "we're in the AI space" but it says nothing about precision, trust, or craft. That's the aesthetic of tools built fast to ride a wave. It's noise.

Surven needs to stand apart from that entirely.

---

## Brand Premise

**The Archival Expert.**

A herbarium doesn't generate plants — it catalogs, identifies, and documents them with precision. The researcher doesn't guess which specimens matter; they measure, track, and record. That's Surven.

Where other "AI tools" feel speculative and generative, Surven feels *rigorous and archival.* We don't predict where you might show up on AI platforms — we track exactly where you do, document what's missing, and execute a precise strategy to fix it. The brand should feel like the expert in the room who has already done the work, not the startup with a bold claim.

**One sentence:** Surven is the agency that treats AI visibility the way a scientist treats a specimen — with precision, documentation, and methodology.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Role | Usage |
|------|-----|------|-------|
| Archival Cream | `#F2EEE3` | Background | Primary surface, page bg, cards |
| Cabinet Ink | `#1A1C1A` | Text / Linework | All body text, borders, UI lines |
| Specimen Sage | `#96A283` | Primary brand color | CTAs, highlights, section accents |
| Rubric Red | `#B54631` | Accent only | Alerts, data callouts, annotation marks |

**Usage rules:**
- Archival Cream is the default canvas. Never use pure white.
- Cabinet Ink replaces black everywhere. It's warmer and more human.
- Specimen Sage is the "this matters" signal — buttons, active states, key metrics.
- Rubric Red is the exception mark. Use it at 2–3% of any composition. If something is red, it's important.
- Dark sections (Cabinet Ink background, Archival Cream text) are used sparingly for maximum contrast — compliance blocks, pricing, feature highlights.

### Typography

**Headline / Display:** Serif — editorial, organic, authoritative
- Recommended: *Freight Display*, *Canela*, *GT Alpina*, or *Cormorant Garamond*
- Character: Classic proportions, slightly literary — not geometric sans, not a startup wordmark font
- Usage: Large section headlines, hero statements, pull quotes

**Body / UI:** Clean sans-serif — readable, invisible, modern
- Recommended: *Inter*, *DM Sans*, or *Geist*
- Character: Neutral, reliable, generous line-height
- Usage: All body copy, labels, navigation, captions, data

**Pairing rule:** Headlines speak. Body text serves. Never let the sans-serif compete for attention.

**Type scale philosophy:** Go large. Wisprflow's headlines take up real estate — they signal confidence. Small type signals uncertainty. Surven should feel like it's not afraid to take up space.

### Design Principles

**1. Generous whitespace.**
Every element breathes. Crowded layouts signal chaos. Open layouts signal expertise.

**2. Editorial hierarchy.**
Large serif headline. One supporting subhead. Then content. Never more than three levels competing on screen.

**3. Data is the design.**
When scan results, rankings, or metrics appear — they *are* the visual. Don't bury data in charts that need a legend. Surface the number. Let it land.

**4. Archival restraint.**
No gradients, no glow, no particle backgrounds. Flat color sections, clean borders, real photography or no photography. If it looks like it was generated by Midjourney, cut it.

**5. Annotation logic.**
Rubric Red should feel like a red pen mark on a document — the thing that needs your attention. Use it only for critical callouts, alerts, or to draw the eye to one thing per view.

## Voice & Tone

### Personality
- Knowledgeable, not arrogant
- Direct, not cold
- Confident, not loud
- Precise, not robotic

### How Surven Talks

**Instead of:**
> "Harness the power of AI to supercharge your visibility across next-gen platforms."

**Surven says:**
> "We track exactly where AI mentions your business — and close the gaps."

**Instead of:**
> "Our cutting-edge algorithm analyzes your presence across AI ecosystems."

**Surven says:**
> "We run a scan, show you where you stand, and build a plan to improve it."

### Voice Rules
- Lead with the specific, not the general. "You ranked in 2 of 4 AI platforms" beats "You have room to grow."
- No em-dashes used dramatically. No ellipses for suspense. Not that kind of brand.
- Short sentences win. One idea per sentence.
- Never use: *revolutionary, cutting-edge, harness, leverage, unlock, supercharge, game-changing.*
- Always use: *track, measure, scan, document, identify, improve, rank.*

## UI / Product Design Language

Surven's Tracker is the product — it needs to look like the brand.

**Dashboard aesthetic:**
- Archival Cream background throughout (not white, not dark mode by default)
- Data cards: clean borders, Cabinet Ink text, no drop shadows or gradients
- Scores and rankings: large type, Specimen Sage for positive, Rubric Red for gaps/missing
- Charts: minimal, flat, labeled clearly — no decorative 3D or animation for its own sake

**Interaction feel:**
- Buttons: solid Specimen Sage, Cabinet Ink text — no rounded pill shapes, slightly squared corners
- States: subtle, no bounce animations — these are professionals looking at data
- Empty states: archival tone — "No scan results yet. Run your first scan." Not "Looks like you haven't started yet! 🚀"

## Brand Applications

### Website
- Hero: Large serif headline on Archival Cream. One sentence. One CTA.
- Navigation: Clean, minimal — product, pricing, about. No mega-menus.
- Social proof section: Real scan screenshots, not illustrated mockups
- Dark section: Pricing or "How It Works" — Cabinet Ink background, Archival Cream text
- Footer: Minimal. No cluttered link grids.

### Sales / Outreach Materials
- Proposal decks: Archival Cream backgrounds, large Specimen Sage section headers, actual client scan data embedded
- Email: Plain text preferred. When designed, same palette — no flashy headers.
- One-pager: Structured like a field report, not a brochure

### Deliverables to Clients
- Scan reports: clean PDF format, archival aesthetic, data front and center
- Monthly updates: consistent template, not a new design each time — builds trust through repetition

---

## What This Rebrand Is Not

- It's not minimalism for minimalism's sake. The archival warmth is intentional — it signals human expertise.
- It's not anti-technology. The brand is precise and data-driven. It just doesn't *look* like a tech startup.
- It's not slow or passive. Confident doesn't mean quiet — Surven should feel decisive and clear.

---

## Summary: The One Thing

If someone looks at Surven's website, report, or any touchpoint and thinks *"these people clearly know what they're doing"* — the rebrand worked.

Everything else (colors, type, whitespace, tone) is just how you get there.
